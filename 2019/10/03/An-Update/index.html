An Update

DevTales
2019-10-03
Ian Ozsvald, Leonard Mlodinow, Micha Gorelick, Personal

Update

I know it’s been a while, but I’ve been extremely busy lately. It’s been a month since I’ve been back in London - I was working as a Student Ambassador at UCL; welcoming students, helping them settle in, showing them around, organising campus tours, etc.

Now that’s all over and I’m finally back to my programming ways. Lectures have started as well and honestly, each and every module this year seems incredibly interesting. I have also been doing a lot of reading in my free time recently and a book that I highly recommend reading is Elastic: Flexible Thinking in a Constantly Changing World by Leonard Mlodinow. In brief, the book talks about how elastic learning and flexible thinking can lead to a multidisciplinary and ever-evolving mind that adapts to today’s dynamic environment. I feel this book will become all the more relevant as time passes by, especially when automation by intelligent systems eliminates the need for menial workers.

Another book I’d strongly recommend - although it is rather technical - is High Performance Python by Ian Ozsvald and Micha Gorelick. Read it, especially if you Python is slow and has poor memory management. This book might change your view. It just might. Looking at you, C/C++ enthusiasts.

See you lot soon!
