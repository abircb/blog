The keys to being a better writer

DevTales
2019-08-22
Writing

Much has been said about the arduous art of writing. Truth is, it is all a lie. There is no “key” to being a better writer. There are however, facts about human brain that you can use to make your writing clearer, succincter, and more readable.

The main technique is keeping things simple. Simple transcends genius. Avoid saying, “The most important thing is” when you can say, “The important thing is”. Because when things are simple, they are clearer to the human brain.

Grab the reader with the first sentence (or the title if you are writing for a digital audience). I rewrote this title several times. It makes you wonder. That’s the point. Know your intention and your rhetoric.

Try to keep things short. Shear the extra wool of your sentences and remove all unnecessary words. I don’t know about you, but that is all my year-9 English teacher used to say, “Prune your sentences, Abir”. Turns out she was not that wrong after all.

Finally, active is better than passive - at least for the human brain (here’s looking at you, Area-51 alien). “I drew that picture” will always be better than “That picture was drawn by me”. That’s just how the human brain works, I guess.
