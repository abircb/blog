The Power of Habit

DevTales
2019-08-05
Charles Duhigg, Habit, Productivity, Systems

This post is NOT about the book, although I strongly recommend reading it if you haven’t.

Habits

To every goal ever set, there is an intention. Behind that intention is motivation. Eventually, some of that motivation fades away. But then, what actually drives you to accomplish that goal is habit.

Habits are algorithms operating in the background that power our lives. Good habits help us reach our goals more effectively and efficiently. Bad ones makes things harder or prevent success entirely. Habits powerfully influence our automatic behavior. Want to get that summer body in 3 months? Then work out 45 mins everyday. Want to read more books? Then read 30 mins everyday. Want to learn a new programming language? Then commit to 30 minutes of programming in that language everyday.

The Power of Habit

"Willpower isn’t just a skill. It’s a muscle, like the muscles in your arms or legs, and it gets tired as it works harder, so there’s less power left over for other things."
- Charles Duhigg wrote in The Power of Habit

Once formed, habits operate automatically. They take otherwise difficult tasks—like saving money—and make them easy. While goals rely on extrinsic motivation, habits are automatic. So when seeking to attain something in our lives, we would do well to invest our time in forming positive habits, rather than concentrating on a specific goal.
